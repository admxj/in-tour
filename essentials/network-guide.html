The Great Firewall

China operates one of the most extensive internet filtering systems in the world, commonly known as the "Great Firewall." Many Western websites and services are blocked, including:

  • Google (Search, Maps, Gmail, Drive)
  • Facebook, Instagram, Twitter
  • YouTube, Netflix
  • WhatsApp, Telegram
  • Many news websites

You will not be able to access these services without a VPN.

Important: Download and set up your VPN before arriving in China. Once you're in China, it may be difficult to access VPN websites to download the apps.

eSIM & Data Options

International Roaming

Check with your home carrier about international roaming plans. US carriers often charge $10-15/day for data in China, which can add up quickly.

eSIM Options

For a more convenient option, consider an eSIM data plan:

  • Airalo: eSIM for 190+ countries, China data from $5
  • eSimple: Global eSIM service
  • Hong Kong eSIM: Often works in mainland China

Physical SIM Cards

You can buy a Chinese SIM card at airports (China Mobile, China Unicom, Telecom). You'll need your passport. Data plans are affordable (¥30-100 for several GB).

What Works Without VPN

Many services function normally in China:

  • Baidu (Chinese search engine)
  • WeChat (messaging + more)
  • Alipay & WeChat Pay
  • Amap (maps—Chinese alternative to Google Maps)
  • Baidu Maps (another map option)
  • Didi (ride-hailing)
  • Trip.com (booking)
  • Baidu Translate or Apple Translate

Tips for Staying Connected

  • Set up before arrival: Install your VPN and apps in your home country
  • Download offline maps: Use Amap or Maps.me for offline navigation
  • Keep multiple options: Have both your VPN and a local data solution
  • Save important info offline: Hotel addresses, train tickets, etc.
