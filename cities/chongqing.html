Why Visit Chongqing

Chongqing is one of China's most unique cities—a mountain metropolis built on dramatic terrain where the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers meet. Known as the "Hot Pot Capital," it's a food lover's paradise with an intense, spicy cuisine that defines the region. The city is also the gateway to Three Gorges and a booming modern urban center with futuristic architecture.

Unlike the flat terrain of most Chinese cities, Chongqing is built on hills with stunning night views, ancient streets, and natural hot springs. The locals are known for their resilience and hospitality, making visitors feel welcome in this vibrant metropolis.

Top Attractions

🌉

Hongya Dong

⏰ 24 hoursFree

A stunning riverside complex built on cliffsides with traditional stilted houses. Best viewed at night when illuminated. The largest tourist attraction in China by visitor count.

🍲

Hot Pot Experience

⏰ 11:00-23:00¥80-200

Chongqing invented the numbing and spicy hot pot. Try Erlang Hot Pot Street or any local restaurant for an authentic experience with málà (numbing-spicy) broth.

🏛️

Three Gorges Museum

⏰ 9:00-17:00Free

Learn about the Three Gorges dam project and the region's history, culture, and natural landscapes through excellent exhibits and artifacts.

🚠

Yangtze River Cable Car

⏰ 7:30-22:00¥20

Cross the Yangtze River by cable car—a unique experience offering panoramic views of the mountain city skyline and the river below.

Suggested Itinerary

2-Day Chongqing Express

Day 1: Hongya Dong → Yangtze River Cable Car → Jiefangbei CBD → Hot pot dinner

Day 2: Three Gorges Museum → Ciqikou Ancient Street → Eling Park (sunset views)

Getting Around

Chongqing's terrain is unique—many streets are interconnected through overpasses and underground pathways. The metro system is extensive and can feel like a roller coaster with underground stations and elevated lines. Taxis and Didi are cheap and convenient.

  • Metro: ¥3-10 per trip, runs 6:30-23:00
  • Taxi: ¥10-40 within city center
  • Cable Car: ¥20 one-way, scenic river crossing
